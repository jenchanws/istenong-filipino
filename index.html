Pambungad

Hindi ko mapigilang di batiin agad ang mga may-akda ng aklat na ito. Una rito, pinalabas nila ang kanilang aklat-patnubay sa pagmamakinilya sa Filipino, na, sa pagkabatid ko'y kauna-unahan ng kanyang uri. Ang kanilang pagsisikap na maihanda at maipalimbag ang panimulang aklat na ito sa istenong Pilipino ay isang makabuluhang ambag sa paglilinang ng wikang pambansa.

Humigit-kumulang sa tatlumpung taon na ngayon simula nang harapin ng Surian ng Wikang Pambansa (SWP) ang popularisasyon ng isteno sa Filipino. Ang hakbang na iyon ay tugon sa kahilingan ng mga hukuman at mga sangguniang bayan. Sa dahilang may iba't ibang matatangkilik nito sa pagsasanay at pagpapalaganap. At napili ang pamamaraan ni Gaudencio Eustaquio na ibinatay sa sistemang Gregg.

Sinimulan ng SWP ang serye ng mga pagsasanay ng mga istenograper sa pamahalaan. Nang lumaon ay tumanggap din ng nagsipanggaling sa mga tanggapang pribado. At nang marami-rami na ang mga nagtapos, ang SWP ay nagtaguod ng mga paligsahang tinangkilik ng pahayagang Taliba at ng Philippine College of Commerce (Polytechnic University of the Philippines ngayon), na naglakip ng kursong istenong Filipino sa kurikulum nito pagkatapos.

Ang Komisyon ng Serbisyo Sibil ay nakapagbigay na nag pagsusulit para sa Senior at Junior Stenographers sa Pilipino. Nakakalat na ngayon sa ilang tanggapang pampamahalaan, mga sangguniang bayan at mga piskalya at hukuman ang mga nagtapos sa sistemang Gregg sa taguyod ng SWP.

Ang aklat na ito'y nagwawagayway ng pamamaraang isinusulong ng SWP. Ang merito ng aklat ay nasa pangyayaring inihanda ng mga subok na mga gurong may karanasan sa sining ng syorthand. Bukod doon, sila'y marunong ng wikang Filipino. Hakbang-hakbang ang kanilang presentasyon ng mga aralin, at ang mga tulong sa pag-aaral ay patungo sa pagtatamo ng kahusayan. Inilalatag nito ang matibay na saligan ng magiging mahusay na istenograper sa wikang pambansa.

Ang Istenong Filipino ay angkop na katambal ng Pagmamakinilya sa Filipino.

Itinatagubilin ko ang paggamit nito sa mga paaralang sekretaryal. At ang pag-aangkin ng sipi ng mga aklatan maging pambayan o pansarili.

PONCIANO B. P. PINEDA, Ph.D.
Direktor, Surian ng Wikang Pambansa

Foreword

I cannot help but immediately congratulate the authors of this book. Before this, they published their guide book on typewriting in Filipino, which, to my understanding, is the first of its kind. Their hard work and dedication to prepare this introductory book on Filipino steno for publication an extremely valuable contribution to the the development of the national language.

It has been approximately thirty years now since the Institute of National Language (INL) has started to tackle the issue of popularizing Filipino steno. That action is a response to the requests of courthouses and municipal councils, for the reason that there are several advantages to training and promoting it, and Gaudencio Eustaquio's method based on the Gregg system has been chosen.

The INL started a series of trainings for stenographers in the government. Afterwards, it also accepted those coming from private offices, and when a significant number of people had graduated, the INL held competitions supported by the Taliba newspaper and the Philippine College of Commerce (now the Polytechnic University of the Philippines), that started to include a course in Filipino steno to its curriculum afterwards.

The Civil Service Commission has been able to provide examinations for Senior and Junior Stenographers in Filipino. These graduates of the Gregg system as supported by the INL have spread throughout several government offices, municipal councils, prosecutors' offices, and courthouses.

This book promotes the INL's method. The merits of this book are in the lessons and activities prepared by teachers with experience in the art of shorthand. Aside from that, they're very knowledgeable in the Filipino language. They present the lessons step by step, and they provide students with guidance towards achieving success. This lays the foundation for being a successful stenographer in the national language.

Filipino Steno is most appropriately used in conjunction with Filipino Typewriting.

I highly recommend this book for use in secretarial studies, and for libraries, national or personal, to obtain a copy of it.

PONCIANO B. P. PINEDA, Ph.D.
Director, Institute of National Language

Foreword

In the present time, as the government is placing much importance on a Filipino-first way of teaching and learning various things relating to people and the environment, any steps towards this outcome will be wholeheartedly accepted by the masses. In schools and offices, in the government and in society, the use of books written in the Filipino language is a very effective component of better understanding by students and citizens. Still, too few books and educational aids have been written in our own language, and even fewer in secretarial and business courses. Due to these circumstances, the publication of Filipino Steno (Introductory Book) by Prof. Fulcida A. Eligado and Prof. Loida A. Yacat is very timely.

Filipino Steno (Introductory Book) is the newest and most modern book on stenography based on the tried-and-tested English system — Gregg Shorthand, Diamond Jubilee Series. Because of this, students will learn this book even more easily, since the concepts and methods used here are no longer new to them. And even those without any knowledge of Filipino stenography will find this easy to learn, since the explanations are simple and help develop critical skills: Writing according to sound, writing short words, phrases and sentences, different types and parts of letters, and articles to provide practice for taking dictations in Filipino. In order to enrich students' minds even further, the book also contains reviews every five lessons. All of the modern methods are designed by the authors for easy understanding by the students.

Filipino Steno (Introductory Book) will also be of great help to those already serving in offices as secretaries and stenographers, because if they incorporate Filipino stenography into their existing skills, they will surely gain even more benefits in their day-to-day work. It is my hope that this type of book will be indispensable to the knowledge and livelihood of the Filipino citizenry.

Preface

FILIPINO STENO, Introductory Book, is an attempt to have a textbook that meets the requirements of students to gain proficiency in shorthand, beyond the requirements of their courses. This book is an aid to teaching and learning steno in our own language.

FILIPINO STENO is based on Gregg Shorthand, Diamond Jubilee Series, an English system already being used by millions. Because the authors tried to simplify the words, phrases and sentences used in letters and articles, those who wish to gain proficiency in secretarial courses will find this easy to learn. This book contains many exercises in order to enrich students' skills towards taking dictation in Filipino.

The book is divided into eight (8) chapters, in which every fifth part is for reviewing principles covered previously. FILIPINO STENO, Introductory Book, can be used for the entire semester. The lessons have been designed to be very useful to students; it also contains word lists, punctuation guidelines and grammar rules that students need.

This book also meets the requirements of those currently serving as secretaries and stenographers in offices, so that they may become more effective and efficient employees. It may also be used as a reference by teachers in the secretarial field.

The authors of this book, FILIPINO STENO, Introductory Book, wish that it may contribute even a little to the expansion of the Filipino language, and to the development and unity of our country, the PHILIPPINES.

Lesson 1

Filipino Steno
Easy to Learn

Filipino Steno is easy to learn. In Shorthand, like in the conventional Filipino writing system, words are written according to their sound or pronunciation. The symbols used in Filipino Steno are taken from Gregg shorthand.

Writing Principles

  1. Vowels A, E, and I.

    The vowel A is represented by a large circle.

    A a

    The vowels E and I are represented by a small circle.

    E, I e
  2. Consonants N, M.

    The N sound is written with a short straight line from the left to the right.

    The M sound is written twice as long as the N.

    N n M m
    ani ane ina ena mana mana
    nene nene ima ema mani mane
    inam enam inin enen anim anem
  3. NG, NGK, and NGG.

    Ng, Ngk, and Ngg are written with a downward diagonal line.

    Ng is half as long as Ngk and Ngg.

    Ngk and Ngg are the same length.

    Ng y Ngk, Ngg Y
    amang amay anggi aYe mangga maYa
    angkan aYan inang enay ngani yane
    angkin aYen ineng eney anang anay
  4. Punctuation and Capital Letters
    period … dash – paragraph ¶
    question mark ? hyphen - parentheses ( )

    The indicator for a capital letter is two short upward dashes under the word.

    Ana ana= Emma ema= Nema nema=
    Annie ane= Mimi meme= Nini nene=

Principles

  1. Consonants B, P, S, and Z.

    B is written with a left-hand downward curve that takes up two consecutive lines.

    P is half the length of B.

    S is a very small right-hand downward curve. It is also written with a left-hand downward curve.

    The stroke for Z is the same as that for S.

    B b P p S, Z S s

    B

    aba aba abang abay bang bay
    abe abe banga baya bangin bayen
    iba eba bangka baYa bangga baYa

    P

    apa apa mapa mapa panga paya
    api ape nipa nepa pingas peyas
    ipa epa pana pana pipa pepa

    S

    sapa sapa basa basa sanga saya
    saba saba sama sama sangga saYa
    pasa pasa sipi sepe angkas aYas
  2. U, K, and G.

    U is an upside down semicircle.

    The consonants K and G are written with an upside down curve from left to right.

    K is an upside down curve half the length of G.

    U u K k G g

    U

    una una umang umay pusa pusa
    upa upa ubas ubas puna puna
    usa usa usap usap busa busa

    K

    kaba kaba kama kama kapa kapa
    baka baka kusa kusa kasa kasa
    abaka abaka kuna kuna uka uka

    G

    aga aga agap agap maga maga
    uga uga ganap ganap baga baga
    siga sega gapang gapay buga buga
  3. AY

    Ay is written with i

    ay i sanay sani agapay agapi
    may mi bagay bagi sungay suyi
    bagay bagi pakay paki kasabay kasabi
  4. Repeated Syllables

    A repeated syllable is written by placing a numeral 1 under the repeated syllable, in words with more than two syllables.

    papasa pa1sa nanaba na1ba unaga uga1
    sasabay sa1bi babasa ba1sa pipiga pe1ga
    panganga paya1 ibaba eba1 sasama sa1ma
  5. Short Forms
    ang a man, mga m ng, nga, nang, ngayon y
    pa, pag p ba b na, ni, niya n
    ka, ko k sa, si s iyo e inyo en

Ikalawang Yugto

Kaunlaran sa Larangan ng Pangkalihiman

Ang mga gawain ng isang makabagong kalihim ay nagmula sa isang payak, konserbatibo, at madaling tungkulin sa maliit na tanggapan, na magmula't kinilala ng lipunan noong 1900 sa Pilipinas. Magmula noon, ang mga gawain at katungkulang ito'y unti-unting lumalawak, nagiging mahalaga dahil sa kahingian ng mga nagbabagong kaayusan at kasiglahan ng mga tanggapan dala ng umuunlad na kabihasnan na pinayaman ng mga kalakalan, kultura, agham at teknolohiya.

At sapagkat, ang larangan ng kalihiman ay isa nang mapagkukunan ng ikabubuhay, ito'y pinagyaman ng mga taong may magandang kaalaman, kabihasaan, at kaparaanan, upang ang mga tanggapan ay matauhan ng mga kalihim na lubhang maalam sa isteno at pagmamakinilya sa Ingles at Pilipino, may kakayahang magpakilos ng mga modernong makinang pang-tanggapan, marunong at masinop sa pagtatago ng mga rekord, at maalan na gumawa at gumamit ng mga makabagong porma't papeles sa kaniyang tanggapan.

Ito nga ang larawan ng kaunlaran sa larangan ng kalihiman, at ito'y makakikita sa mga malalaking tanggapan, umaalalay at kompidante sa mga tagapamahala ng kompanya, may mga makabagong pananamit at sumasahod ng malaki. Sila'y nagsimula sa pagiging isang klerk, pagkatapos naging stenograper, o kumpletong kalihim at ngayon isa nang ganap na puno ng mga tauhan sa kalihiman. Ganito sila natataas ng tungkulin, at ganyan sila umuunlad sa gawaing pagkalihim sa tanggapan.
